Pataz Rigging Toolz

Welcome to the manual for Pataz Rigging Toolz! This guide will walk you through every feature of the addon, designed to solve the most common frustrations in the character creation pipeline so you can focus on creativity, not repetitive technical tasks.

All tools are conveniently located in the Rigging Tab in the 3D Viewport's sidebar (press the N key to toggle visibility). The panels are context-aware and will only appear when a relevant object is selected.

What is a "Character"?

Before we dive in, let's clarify what the addon considers a "character." For the Pataz Rigging Toolz panels to appear, your selected object must be associated with an armature rig in one of the following ways:

  • It is an Armature object itself.

  • It is directly parented to an Armature.

  • It has an Armature Modifier (for meshes, curves, etc.) or a Grease Pencil Armature Modifier that links it to a rig.

If the panels aren't showing up, this is the first thing to check! Make sure your meshes and other objects are correctly linked to your character's rig.

The Non-Destructive Rigify Workflow

This is the core of the addon—a workflow that lets you rig without fear. Standard Rigify is destructive; any custom constraints, drivers, or tweaks made to a generated rig are permanently lost if you need to regenerate. Pataz Rigging Toolz fixes this.

The new workflow is simple:

  1. Generate: Create your rig from the metarig as usual.

  2. Customize: Perfect your setup directly on the generated rig. Add constraints, create drivers, tweak bone shapes, and assign bone collections.

  3. Edit: Make any necessary changes to your metarig.

  4. Regenerate: Use the Generate Rig button with the "Preserve" options enabled. The addon intelligently saves your custom work from Step 2 and reapplies it to the newly generated rig.

Main Rigify Controls

When you select a metarig or a generated Rigify rig, this panel appears, giving you central control over the generation process.

  • Generate Rig: This supercharged operator functions like the standard Rigify generator but integrates the powerful "Preserve" options. When regenerating, it saves and reapplies your custom work from the existing rig.

  • Rig ⇄ Metarig: Instantly swap between your metarig and its generated rig. The addon handles hiding, showing, and selecting the correct armature for a seamless, one-click context switch.

Regeneration Options: Preserve Your Work

This section is where the magic happens. Before regenerating, enable the toggles for any properties you have customized on the generated rig to ensure they are preserved.

Generate Options

  • Keep Pose: Retains the character's current pose upon regeneration.

  • Keep Animation: Re-links the existing Action (animation data) to the new rig.

  • Hide Metarig: Automatically hides the metarig after generation, selecting the new rig.

  • Show Deform Bones: Displays the deformation bone collections after generation for immediate skinning review.

✅ The "Preserve" Toggles

Enable these to save and restore specific properties that you've modified on the generated rig's bones.

  • Transform Locks: Keeps the transform channel locks you've set.

  • Rotation Mode: Retains any per-bone rotation mode changes.

  • Bendy Bones: Preserves all Bendy Bone settings configured on the generated rig.

  • Relations: Restores parenting, Inherit Scale, and other relation properties.

  • Bone Collections: Keeps your custom bone collection assignments.

  • Inverse Kinematics: Reapplies custom IK settings from the generated bones.

  • Deform: Retains the Deform property on bones, ensuring skinning is unaffected.

  • Viewport Display: Keeps bone visibility, colors, and display settings.

  • Custom Shape: Saves custom bone shapes and their transforms.

  • Bone Constraints: The powerhouse feature. Preserves the entire constraint stack on each bone. Add, edit, or reorder constraints on the generated rig, and your setup will be perfectly rebuilt.

  • Custom Properties: Restores any custom properties and their drivers that you've added directly to the generated rig's bones.

Rigging Utilities & Workflow Tools

Once your rig is generated, this panel becomes your daily driver for managing the rig, preparing for animation, and fixing common issues.

  • Clear Pose: Instantly snaps the character to its rest pose. Use the checkboxes to protect specific bone layers (DEF, MCH, ORG) from being reset.

  • Toggle Modifiers: A massive performance booster! Instantly toggle the viewport visibility of heavy modifiers (Subdivision, Cloth, etc.) across all meshes associated with your rig.

  • Show Deform / Solo Deform Bones: Quickly toggle the visibility of your deformation bones. "Solo Deform" hides all other controls, isolating the skinning skeleton for review.

  • Set Rotation Mode: Batch-change the rotation mode for every bone in the rig at once.

  • Reset Constraint Distances: Fixes stretched limbs by resetting the rest_length on 'Stretch To' and 'Limit Distance' constraints.

  • Set BBones Scale: Tame your B-Bones with a single slider to control their display thickness throughout the rig.

  • Edit Custom Shape: A huge workflow accelerator. In Pose Mode, select a bone and click this button to instantly enter Edit Mode for its custom shape. Click it again to return to posing. Supports editing multiple custom shapes at once!


Weighting Tools: Master Your Skinning Workflow 🎨

A focused toolset to make the tedious process of weight painting faster and more intuitive.

  • Toggle Weight Paint: Your one-button setup for the perfect skinning environment. It can automatically:

    • Enable armature X-Ray for clear visibility.

    • Display the mesh wireframe over solid faces.

    • Show or solo the deformation bones.

    • Isolate the rig and mesh from the scene (Local View).

    • Set the rig to its rest position for accurate painting.

    • Toggling it off cleanly reverts all settings.

  • Transfer Vertex Groups: Copy weights from your active mesh to other selected meshes (like clothing). The Transfer Mode gives you full control:

    • Auto: The smartest choice. It attempts a perfect copy using topology and falls back to projection if the meshes are different.

    • Topology: For exact copies between identical mesh structures.

    • Interpolated: For projecting weights between different meshes.

  • Merge Vertex Groups: Combine weight groups to clean up your skinning. In Pose Mode, select the bones to merge from, then select the target bone last (making it active). This tool merges their weights into the active bone's group and removes the old groups.

  • Clean Vertex Groups: A bundle of cleanup tools for optimizing weights:

    • Normalize Weights: Ensures all influences on a vertex add up to 1.0.

    • Remove Zero Weights: Cleans groups by removing vertices with no influence.

    • Remove Unused Groups: Deletes empty vertex groups that clutter your list.

    • Limit Total: Crucial for game engines! Limits how many bones can influence a single vertex.

Cleanup Crew: Keep Your Scenes Tidy 🧹

This panel is your toolkit for decluttering and optimizing models. Bad or orphaned data can increase file sizes and cause export errors. These one-click operators safely remove it from all selected objects.

  • Clear Vertex Groups: Deletes all vertex groups.

  • Clear/Apply Shape Keys: Removes all shape keys. The Apply version bakes the current mix into the base mesh.

  • Clear UV Maps: Deletes all UV layers.

  • Clear Color Attributes: Removes all vertex color data.

  • And more... Includes quick fixes for clearing custom normals, sculpt masks, skin data, and other temporary data layers.

  • Lock Transforms: Protects objects from being accidentally moved, rotated, or scaled.
